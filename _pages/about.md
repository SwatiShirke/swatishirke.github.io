I am a graduate student researcher in Robotics Engineering at Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI), specializing in Robot Control and Motion Planning. I am currently involved with the Aerial-Robot Control and Perception (ACP) Lab at WPI.

In my current research, I am developing a formwork for an aerial delivery system employing multiple quadrotors, utilizing Model Predictive Control (MPC) for effective trajectory and position control. A critical aspect of this work is ensuring collision avoidance between quadrotors and obstacles by embedding constraints directly into the MPC framework. The project also explores system redundancy by navigating Cartesian space through null-space utilization. Additionally, a cable-attached mechanism for payload lifting is incorporated, with an advanced cable model designed to prevent cable crossovers. This research includes the development of a generic simulator using ROS and Python, intended to serve as a resource for both academic and research purposes.

In my research on safety-critical control and planning for autonomous vehicle fleets using multi-agent Deep Reinforcement Learning, we achieved a 97% success rate in collision avoidance during lane-merging scenarios, with an average traffic speed of 23.35 and a standard deviation of 1.43. In intersection scenarios, we attained a 67% success rate, with an average speed of 21.14 and a standard deviation of 1.82.

My coursework and projects have given me valuable exposure to Robot Control and Motion Planning. Through my coursework, I developed controllers for quadrotors, mobile robots, autonomous vehicles, and manipulators using Python, C++, ROS, and MATLAB-Simulink. I also implemented various motion planning algorithms for both holonomic and non-holonomic systems, enhancing my ability to address complex control and navigation challenges across diverse robotic platforms.

I have studied and implemented both classical control techniques, such as PD and PID, and modern control methods, including state space and optimal control. My skills encompass state-space design, kinematic and dynamic system modelling, and system analysis. I developed trajectory control for an autonomous vehicle using a 4-wheel dynamic state-space model and applied Model Predictive Control (MPC). Additionally, I built a 3-DOF mobile robot from scratch using ROS1 and developed a motor control library in C++. In this case, I implemented a PID controller on an Arduino, and integrated it with ROS1 on a BeagleBone Black.  I also developed a PD controller and visual servo control for robotic manipulators. Furthermore, I designed an LQR controller for a quadrotor, achieving precise thrust control and effective disturbance handling.

As part of Motion Planning coursework, I implemented Improved Conflict-Based Search (ICBS), Hybrid
A*, and PRM planners to for holonomic and non-holonomic robots, differential drive robots and a vehicle
with Ackermann steering model.


I have expertise in system design, robot programming, control systems, motion planning, and data structuring, with proficiency in ROS, C++, Python, MATLAB, and Linux operating systems. Additionally, I have hands-on experience in hardware-software integration, working with embedded systems, including BLDC motors, relays, SONAR, IMU, cameras, and IR sensors, as well as platforms such as Arduino, AVR, and BeagleBone Black.

My journey in the field of Robotics began with a Cognitive Robotics and Intelligent System Lab (CRISTL) at Vishwakarma Institute of Technology, India. Here, I worked on Robotic Control, Motion Planning and  Sensor Data Processing. Further, I worked on [ROS-based Cognitive Robot](https://github.com/SwatiShirke/Robotics-/tree/master/ROS%20Based%20Cognitive%20Robot) as part of my dissertation project. 

After my BTech, I worked for five years as a PEGA Application Developer at Vodafone India Services Pvt Ltd, starting as a graduate engineer trainee and progressing to Deputy Manager. I initially focused on Proof of Concept (POC) projects, earning the Extra Mile Champion Award for generating business opportunities. As a junior developer, I automated 12 critical business modules. Later, I led a team of six junior developers, overseeing technical design, impact analysis, work estimation, distribution, and timely deliveries, which improved team efficiency by 8%. I was also recognized as a Vodafone Star in November 2021 and July 2020 for successful module deliveries.
